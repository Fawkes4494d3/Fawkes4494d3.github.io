    • So 144 it is.
      • 144 is composed of 3 individually-perfect-square digits, 1,4 and 4
      • Is a perfect square itself, as is it's reverse 441.
      • Sum of it's digits=S(144)=1+4+4=9 and product of it's digits=P(144)=1*4*4=16 are also perfect squares
      • S(144)=32 and P(144)=42, and
        144=122=(3*4)2=3242=S(144)*P(144)
        so 144 is the product of the sum of it's digits and the product of it's digits.
      • 144 is 12 many 12s, the significant number mentioned in my previous post, appearing in many theological contexts (of course XD)
      • A property which sounds quite fun to state as I ponder over it is:
        sum of any nth powers of the digits of 144 is divisible by 3
        i.e. 1n+4n+4n is divisible by 3 for any whole number n.
